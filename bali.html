Welcome to Bali

Discover the magical island of Bali with its stunning temples, lush green rice terraces, and pristine beaches. This tropical paradise offers a perfect blend of spiritual culture, natural beauty, and warm hospitality that captures the hearts of travelers worldwide.

Top Attractions in Bali

Uluwatu Temple

Uluwatu Temple, Bali Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most iconic and sacred sea temples, perched dramatically on a steep cliff 70 meters above the Indian Ocean. This 11th-century temple is not only a significant spiritual site but also offers breathtaking sunset views that are considered among the best in the world. Visitors can witness traditional Kecak fire dances performed at sunset, adding to the magical atmosphere. The temple is surrounded by lush forests inhabited by playful monkeys, creating a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and spiritual significance that makes Uluwatu a must-visit destination in Bali.

Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Tegallalang Rice Terraces are among the most famous and photographed landscapes in Bali. Located north of Ubud, these stunning emerald-green rice paddies are carved into the hillsides in a traditional Balinese irrigation system known as "subak." The terraces offer breathtaking panoramic views, especially in the early morning when the light creates beautiful shadows and highlights. Visitors can walk through the narrow paths between the paddies, learn about traditional farming methods, and enjoy fresh coconut drinks at the many viewpoints. The Tegallalang Rice Terraces represent the perfect harmony between human creativity and natural beauty that defines Bali's cultural landscape.

Ubud Monkey Forest

Ubud Monkey Forest is a sacred nature reserve and temple complex in the heart of Ubud, home to over 700 long-tailed Balinese macaques. This lush tropical forest spans approximately 12.5 hectares and features ancient Hindu temples dating back to the 14th century, creating a unique blend of natural and cultural preservation. Visitors can walk along stone paths through dense jungle, observe the monkeys in their natural habitat, and learn about conservation efforts. The forest is not only a popular tourist attraction but also an important site for research and education, representing Bali's commitment to preserving both its wildlife and cultural heritage.
